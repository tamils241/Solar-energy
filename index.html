Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about going solar with Stackly.

Most homeowners save between 40-70% on their electricity bills after installing solar panels. The exact savings depend on your location, energy consumption, system size, and local utility rates. On average, our customers recoup their investment within 5-8 years and enjoy free electricity for decades after. Use our free quote calculator to estimate your personal savings.

Most residential installations are completed in 1-3 days. The entire process from initial consultation to grid connection typically takes 4-8 weeks, which includes site assessment, system design, permitting, installation, and utility approval. We handle all the paperwork to make it hassle-free for you.

We offer multiple financing options including cash purchases, solar loans with $0 down, leases, and Power Purchase Agreements (PPAs). Many customers qualify for zero-down financing with immediate monthly savings. You may also be eligible for the 30% federal tax credit and state-level incentives that can significantly reduce your upfront cost.

Solar panels still generate electricity on cloudy days, just at reduced efficiency (typically 10-25% of full capacity). At night, your system draws power from the grid (if grid-tied) or from your battery storage system. With net metering, you can export excess daytime energy and earn credits to offset nighttime usage.

Yes! We stand behind our work with comprehensive warranties: 25-year performance warranty on solar panels, 10-15 year warranty on inverters, 10-year workmanship warranty on installation, and 25-year warranty on battery storage systems. We also offer optional extended maintenance plans.

Solar panels can be installed on most roof types including asphalt shingle, metal, tile, and flat roofs. Our team conducts a thorough site assessment to evaluate your roof's condition, orientation, shading, and structural integrity. We'll design a custom system that maximizes your energy production regardless of roof type.

Solar panels require minimal maintenance. Rain naturally cleans most dust and debris, but we recommend professional cleaning once or twice a year in dry climates. Our monitoring system tracks performance 24/7, and we'll alert you if anything needs attention. With our maintenance plan, we handle all cleaning, inspections, and repairs.

Net metering is a billing arrangement that credits you for excess electricity your solar panels send back to the grid. When your system produces more power than you use, your meter spins backward and you earn credits at the retail rate. You can then use those credits when your system isn't producing (at night or during cloudy periods).

Today's premium solar panels achieve efficiency ratings of 20-23%, meaning they convert that much sunlight into usable electricity. While that number may seem low, a typical home installation with 20-25 panels generates more than enough power for the average household. Our panels use advanced monocrystalline cells with anti-reflective coating for maximum energy capture.

A battery is optional but increasingly recommended. Without a battery, you'll still save money through net metering, but you won't have backup power during outages. With a solar battery, you can store excess energy for nighttime use, protect against power outages, and maximize your energy independence. Many customers choose to start solar-only and add batteries later.
